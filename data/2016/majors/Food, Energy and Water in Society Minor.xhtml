The Food, Energy and Water in Society minor is designed to offer an education component to all University of Nebraska students that will prepare them with the knowledge and skills to analyze complex, real-world problems from a systems perspective to make informed decisions regarding current and emerging food, energy, and water issues, and the interrelatedness of agriculture, natural resources, and society.

MAJOR REQUIREMENTS

A minimum 18 credit hour minor in Food, Energy, Water in Society Minor will include 12 required courses and six hours of required course work at the 300 and 400 level.

Required Courses: 18

AGRI/NRES 103 Introduction to agricultural and Natural Resource Systems or NRES 111 Natural Resource Conservation in Society 3

ALEC 108 Food Systems or ALEC 125 Land, Food and People 3

ENSC 110 Energy in Perspective or ENSC 220 Introduction to Energy Systems or ENSC 230 Energy, Economics & the Environment 3

SCIL 109 Water in Society 3

SCIL 300 Experiential Learning in Food, Energy and Water Systems I 3

SCIL 400 Experiential Learning in Food, Energy, and Water Systems II 3
